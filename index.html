About Me

Due to my upbringing, love for biodiversity is deeply ingrained in me. Since my childhood, I have been conscious about the flora and fauna around me. My compassion for the animal world dates to when I was merely ten years old. Over the years, from crawling to running, holding a leash in my hands, I was taught to fall and rise again, by a patient furry friend. Growing up, I was surrounded by many pets, and I realized my passionate inclination for working in the field of animal conservation during 2nd year of graduation itself. During my graduation, I have actively involved myself in several curricular and co-curricular activities, including volunteering for several projects. I have been a Technical Head of the environmental club at Ramanujan College, University of Delhi. I also got the privilege to work under an international organization like WWF-India for a period of 4 months. My most recent engagement was with the Wildlife Institute of India (WII). It has been a great privilege to work under the institution and learn throughout the journey. During my engagement, I worked for the project “Intensive Monitoring Source Population of Tiger, Co Predator & Prey” at Kanha Tiger Reserve, and carried out fieldwork related to camera trapping for phase IV - Tiger monitoring project. I assisted during the training of forest guards in the phase IV monitoring protocol for tiger population estimation. I have the ability to conduct field surveys and the knowledge to conform to the environmental laws and regulations associated with the wildlife in each area. My training includes working in laboratories conducting research studies using the data that’s collected when in the field. My experience includes preparing technical reports, performing environmental analysis and studying wild animals within their natural habitats. Presently I seek an opportunity to work with the experts in the field of wildlife conservation and management to learn more in detail, the methodologies involved, and different aspects in this field. Most importantly, I am looking forward to gain field experiences to align my theoretical knowledge with the practical challenges. Working with your department will not only provide me with an opportunity to explore a new landscape, but will also expose me to the practical aspects of a new discipline of science, i.e. behavioral biology, which will eventually help me in narrowing down my research interests for my PhD.  I am self-motivated, committed and capable of functioning independently as well as in a team. I am always keen to learn new things and I am enthusiastic for new challenges. I am sure given my persisting nature, openness to experiences, working in the remote and challenging field conditions and my desire to continuously learn, I will be a valuable addition to your institution. Thank you for your time.
